About Us

We are a dedicated team of volunteers committed to providing first aid and patient care at community events.

At Division 59, we believe in the power of first aid to prevent and alleviate human suffering. Our volunteers are at the heart of our mission, providing essential first aid services, emergency response, and healthcare support at local events, public gatherings, and in times of crisis.

Our commitment extends beyond emergency response. We are passionate about education and empowerment, offering comprehensive first aid and health care training to individuals and organizations, fostering a community ready and equipped to respond to emergencies.

Our legacy is built on the pillars of community service, volunteerism, and education. As we look to the future, St. John Ambulance Division 59 continues to innovate and adapt, striving to meet the evolving health needs of our communities and make a lasting impact on the lives we touch.


Training

At Division 59, we believe in empowering our volunteers with comprehensive knowledge and skills to ensure they are fully equipped to serve the community effectively. Our robust training program is designed to meet the needs of both new and experienced volunteers, encompassing a wide range of essential topics and practical experiences.

Weekly Training Meetings

Our division holds weekly meetings that are pivotal to our training regimen. These gatherings are a blend of:

  • Hands-on Training: Practical sessions where members apply their knowledge in real-life scenarios, enhancing their proficiency in first aid and emergency response techniques.
  • Medical Lectures: Presentations and discussions led by healthcare professionals, covering a broad spectrum of medical topics to deepen our members' understanding of various health conditions and treatment modalities.
  • Case Studies: Interactive analysis of real-world cases, allowing members to critically assess and discuss response strategies, fostering a culture of continuous learning and improvement.

Advanced Medical First Responder Course

One of the highlights of our training program is the Advanced Medical First Responder Course, an intensive course designed to equip volunteers with the skills necessary to provide advanced first aid and life support in emergency situations. This course is offered free of charge for our members.

Join Us

Are you ready to make a difference? Join the ranks of St. John Ambulance Division 59 and be part of a distinguished group of volunteers who are changing lives every day. Whether you're seeking to learn life-saving skills, give back to your community, or become part of a supportive network of like-minded individuals, your journey starts here.

Why Join?

  • Learn and Lead: Gain invaluable first aid and healthcare knowledge through our accredited training programs. Empower yourself with the skills to save lives and the opportunity to lead community initiatives.
  • Serve Your Community: Be on the frontline of care, providing essential support at local events, during emergencies, and wherever there's a need. Your actions can be the difference between life and death.
  • Belong to a Global Movement: Join a global network of volunteers committed to the cause of humanity. Experience the solidarity of working with a team that values compassion, respect, and integrity.

How to Join:

Becoming a member is a straightforward process:

  1. Express Interest: Fill out our online application form to express your interest.
  2. Attend an Orientation: Learn more about our mission, values, and the roles available through an orientation session.
  3. Complete Training: Depending on your role, complete the necessary first aid or healthcare training to equip you with the skills needed to serve effectively.
  4. Get Involved: Start volunteering at events, participate in training sessions, and become an integral part of our community outreach efforts.

At Division 59, we welcome individuals from all walks of life. No prior experience is necessary, only the desire to learn and contribute. Join us today and be part of a noble cause. Together, we can save lives and build resilient communities.
